About Me

I am a Master's student in Computer Science at Johns Hopkins University, specializing in Human Language Technology. My research focuses on developing and evaluating Large Language Models for scientific research, particularly in astrophysics.

I'm passionate about bridging the gap between AI capabilities and domain expert needs, working at the intersection of natural language processing, machine learning interpretability, and scientific applications. I'm also interested in AI safety and the social ethics of technology.

Selected Projects

Testing Social Reasoning in LLMs

Spring 2026

Designed and evaluated a benchmark of 40 original scenarios spanning Theory of Mind, Pragmatics, and Chain-of-Thought social reasoning across 12 state-of-the-art LLMs (4B–671B parameters). Administered a human baseline survey (n=12) to anchor model performance against human-level social reasoning. Found that ToM and Pragmatics competence are dissociated at the model level, and that the dominant failure mode is a collapse of epistemic boundaries between observer and character knowledge.

Regional Variation in ChatGPT Use Across U.S. States

Spring 2026

Applied BERTopic to 68,668 first-turn English user prompts from the WildChat corpus to study geographic and socioeconomic variation in ChatGPT use across 36 U.S. states. Found that all 76 raw topics vary significantly across states, and that higher education and income correlate with specialized creative uses while generic image-generation requests are more prevalent in lower-education and lower-income states.
